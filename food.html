Delhi

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a signature dish of North India. Marinated chicken is grilled and cooked in a creamy, spiced tomato gravy. Served with naan or rice, it’s loved for its rich, buttery flavor and mild spices.

Maharashtra

Vada Pav

Vada Pav, the "Indian burger," is a spicy potato fritter in a bread bun, served with tangy chutneys and dry garlic powder. A street food staple across Mumbai, it's fast, flavorful, and a cultural icon in Maharashtra.

Odisha

Pakhala Bhata

Pakhala is fermented rice soaked in water, often eaten with curd, green chilies, and fried vegetables. A summer staple in Odisha, it’s known for its cooling effect and digestive benefits. Celebrated every year on "Pakhala Dibasa."

Bihar

Litti Chokha

Litti Chokha is a wholesome meal with wheat dough balls filled with roasted gram flour, served with mashed spiced vegetables. Baked over fire or in ghee, it reflects the earthy taste of Bihari cuisine.

Telangana

Hyderabadi Biryani

Hyderabadi Biryani is slow-cooked using the "dum" method, with layers of saffron-flavored rice and marinated meat. A royal dish from the Nizams' kitchens, it’s rich in spices and often served with raita or salan.

Punjab

Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a spicy chickpea curry paired with fluffy deep-fried bread. Popular across Punjab and Delhi, it's a hearty and indulgent meal often enjoyed with onions, pickles, and curd.

Tamil Nadu

Masala Dosa

Dosa is a crispy crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, usually stuffed with spicy mashed potatoes (masala dosa). Served with coconut chutney and sambar, it’s a staple of South Indian cuisine.

Rajasthan

Dal Baati Churma

Dal Baati Churma is a Rajasthani trio: spicy lentils, baked wheat balls soaked in ghee, and sweet crushed baatis. A royal, filling dish that reflects the flavors of Rajasthan’s desert tradition.

Gujarat

Dhokla

Dhokla is a steamed savory cake made from fermented gram flour. Soft and spongy, it’s tempered with mustard seeds and garnished with coriander and coconut. Served with chutneys, it's a healthy and beloved snack.

West Bengal

Ilish Bhapa

Ilish Bhapa is a Bengali delicacy made with hilsa fish steamed in a mustard paste. Rich, aromatic, and slightly pungent, it’s best enjoyed with plain rice—especially during the monsoon season.
